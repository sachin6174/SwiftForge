# Privacy Policy for SwiftForge

**Last Updated:** July 4, 2026

**SwiftForge** ("the App") is developed as an offline-first, native macOS application designed to help developers practice Data Structures, Algorithms, and Swift iOS engineering concepts.

Your privacy is paramount to us. This Privacy Policy details how SwiftForge handles your data.

---

### 1. Data Collection & Storage

#### 1.1 Local Data Persistence
SwiftForge operates entirely locally on your Apple device:
- **Code Drafts & Solved Badges**: Saved locally on your device's filesystem (e.g., `user_activity.json`).
- **Activity & Streaks**: Daily usage streaks are calculated and stored locally on your device.
- **Console Logs**: Local logs are stored on your device's filesystem (`app_test.log`).

#### 1.2 Zero Personal Data Telemetry
- SwiftForge **does not collect, transmit, store, or share** any personally identifiable information (PII), telemetry, analytics, tracking cookies, or user identity data to external servers.
- No user account creation or login registration is required to use SwiftForge.

---

### 2. Third-Party Services & Network Requests

- **Swift Practice Track**: Optional practice questions (such as HTTP GET/POST requests) connect directly to standard public mock REST APIs (e.g., `https://jsonplaceholder.typicode.com`) solely for demonstration and network testing purposes when initiated by you.
- SwiftForge does not transmit user code, analytics, or personal metrics to any third-party marketing or tracking vendors.

---

### 3. Children's Privacy

SwiftForge does not knowingly collect any personal information from children under the age of 13 (or applicable local age limit). The application contains no ads, in-app purchases, or social tracking tools.

---

### 4. Data Security

All user code drafts, test suite evaluations, and daily streak counts remain isolated within your macOS local sandbox and user application data folder.

---

### 5. Changes to This Privacy Policy

We may update our Privacy Policy periodically. Any updates will be published within the application repository and release notes.
